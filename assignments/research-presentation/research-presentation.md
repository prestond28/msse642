## What the data is actually saying

It's been confirmed with data that AI is negatively affecting the job market for younger software developers. A Stanford Digital Economy Lab study using ADP payroll data showed that software developer employment for ages 22 to 25 fell roughly 20% from its late 2022 peak by July 2025. Indeed's Hiring Lab data backs this up from the demand side: software development job postings were down 36.4% versus the pre-pandemic February 2020 baseline as of October 2025, with junior/standard titles falling 34% versus senior titles down only 19%. Another staggering statistic is that 37% of employers said they'd rather "hire" AI than a recent graduate, and of those graduates who do get hired, 60% had been fired within a year as of 2024.

There is a counter, however, because research done by SignalFire (reported in TechCrunch's article) argues the "AI killed junior jobs" saying is overstated because engineers made up 55% of all new hires in 2025 across 12 major tech companies. TechCrunch also quotes the Nvidia CEO Jansen Huang: “Somebody said that AI is going to destroy all of the software engineering jobs" but he argues against that saying, "Number one, the software engineers who know how to work with AI ... are the most popular and the most successful. Number two, the software engineers are busier than ever."
